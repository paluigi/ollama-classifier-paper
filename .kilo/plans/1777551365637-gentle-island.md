### 8. NEW: Confidence Analysis paragraph + Table 3 (insert after Effect of Subclass Definitions, before Practical implications)

**New paragraph** "Confidence and calibration." with these findings:
1. Both models show statistically significant positive correlation between confidence and correctness (BART r≈0.35, Ollama r≈0.40–0.45, all p<0.001).
2. Ollama mean confidence for correct predictions is 0.954 vs BART's 0.532. Even Ollama's incorrect predictions average 0.820 — higher than BART's correct predictions.
3. Score gap for misclassified items: Ollama 0.92 vs BART 0.46. Ollama's errors are more "decisive" — the model strongly prefers the wrong label over the correct one.
4. Practical implication: confidence thresholds are informative for both models, but Ollama's compressed high-confidence range means finer-grained triage (e.g., examining the score gap) may be more effective than a simple threshold.

**New Table 3** (`tab:confidence`) with columns: Variation, Conf (correct), Conf (incorrect), Corr. r, Score Gap (mean).
